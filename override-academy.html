Part 1: Drivetrain & Chassis Build

Step 1: Chassis Frame

The Hero Bot Flex uses a rectangular aluminum C-channel chassis. Start by building the main frame using 2× 35-hole C-channels for the sides and 2× 25-hole C-channels for front and rear connections. Use 8-32 screws with nylock nuts at all structural joints.

🔑 Key Note

Always use shaft collars on every axle to prevent lateral movement. Missing shaft collars are the #1 cause of drivetrain failure at competitions.

Step 2: Drive Wheels & Motors

Mount 4-inch Omni wheels on the front and back with 3.25-inch traction wheels in the middle for optimal traction. Connect 4× V5 Smart Motors using the blue 600 RPM cartridge. Wire motors to ports 1, 10, 11, 20 for the standard tank drive configuration.

Step 3: Encoder Sensors

Install Rotation Sensors on both sides of the drivetrain. These are critical for autonomous tracking. Mount them on dedicated axles connected to the drive wheels with no slippage.

Wiring Checklist

  • Left front motor → Port 1 (reversed)
  • Left rear motor → Port 2 (reversed)
  • Right front motor → Port 10 (forward)
  • Right rear motor → Port 11 (forward)
  • Inertial Sensor → Port 21
  • Left rotation sensor → Port 3
  • Right rotation sensor → Port 9

Step 4: Gear Ratios

The Hero Bot Flex uses a direct drive configuration with 600 RPM motors and 4-inch wheels, giving a theoretical max speed of ~6.28 feet per second. This balances speed and torque for competitive play.

⚠️ Common Mistake

Do not over-tighten axle screws — this causes motor burnout. Check that all axles spin freely by hand before turning on the robot.

Part 2: Intake, Scoring & Autonomous Systems

Intake System Build

The Flex intake uses 3-inch Flex Wheels on a 1/2-inch hex shaft. Build the intake arms from 15-hole C-channels angled at 30° outward for optimal Cup capture. Two motors run the intake rollers at 200 RPM for reliable, consistent picking.

Intake Motor Configuration

  • Left intake → Port 3 (forward)
  • Right intake → Port 8 (reversed)
  • Use green 200 RPM cartridges for torque
  • Set intake to run continuously during driver control

Cup Flipper Mechanism

A 4-bar linkage connects to a 1-motor lift system that flips Cups into Goals. The 4-bar uses 1× 35-hole and 1× 25-hole C-channels on each side, synchronized by a connecting bar across the back.

💡 Pro Tip

Calibrate the 4-bar at the start of every match. Use a limit switch or rotation sensor to set the "home" position automatically during autonomous initialization.

Toggle Flipper

A pneumatic piston or small motor arm can flip Toggles. The Hero Bot Flex uses a 1-motor arm with a 5:1 gear reduction to generate enough torque to flip Toggles reliably even from awkward angles.

Autonomous System

The V5 Brain runs VEXcode Pro V5 with PROS or VEXcode Blocks. The standard autonomous uses:

  • Inertial sensor for accurate turning (±1°)
  • Rotation sensors for precise distance tracking
  • PID controllers for smooth, accurate movement
  • Vision sensor (optional) for goal alignment

Standard 15-Second Autonomous Routine

  1. Score pre-loaded Cup into Alliance Goal (3 pts)
  2. Drive to Neutral Goal and score 1 Cup (2 pts)
  3. Flip nearest Toggle (points vary)
  4. Return to Alliance Zone for protection

Match Strategy with Hero Bot Flex

🟢 Standard Match Flow

Autonomous: Score pre-loaded Cup, secure Autonomous Bonus. Early Driver: Cycle Cups to Alliance Goal aggressively for first 60 seconds. Mid-Match: Contest Neutral Goals and deny opponent access. Endgame: Flip Toggles, secure Midfield zone if possible.

🔵 Defensive Strategy

Post your robot at the Neutral Goal zone in mid-match to prevent the opponent from scoring while your partner runs the offensive cycle. Requires constant communication via controller rumble or hand signals.

🔴 High-Score Strategy

Maximize Cup cycling speed — target 8+ Cups scored per match. Focus on Alliance Goal (secure first), then Neutral Goals. Reserve 20 seconds for Toggle flips and Midfield positioning.

🔧 Priority Upgrades

1. Faster Intake Rollers

Switch to blue 600 RPM cartridges on intake with a 2:1 gear reduction. Increases pick-up speed by ~40% for faster Cup cycling.

2. Pneumatic Toggle Arm

Replace the motor toggle arm with a pneumatic piston for instant, reliable Toggle flipping in endgame. Much faster than motor-driven.

3. Side Guards / Bumpers

Add polycarbonate side panels to protect intake rollers from defensive hits during match play. Critical for elimination rounds.

4. Improved Autonomous Sensors

Add a second Inertial Sensor and GPS Sensor for dramatically improved autonomous accuracy and repositioning capabilities.

⚡ Advanced Modifications

5. Double Cup Holder

Extend the Cup channel to hold 2 Cups simultaneously. Doubles scoring efficiency — essential for high-level competition teams.

6. Adjustable Intake Angles

Add a second motor to adjust intake arm height for picking up Cups from different field positions and angles.

7. Drive Weight Reduction

Replace steel structural components with aluminum wherever possible. Every pound saved improves acceleration and maneuverability.

8. Vision Sensor Goal Alignment

Mount Vision Sensor on intake to automatically detect and align with Goals during autonomous for consistent high-accuracy scoring.

STRATEGY HUB

♟️ Override Strategy Playbook

Beginner to championship-level strategies for every aspect of Override.

🟢 Beginner

Focus on Alliance Goal First

Your Alliance Goal is worth the most reliable points. Score Pins and Cups in your Alliance Goal consistently throughout the match. Protect it by keeping your robot nearby during key moments. This simple strategy can win many qualification matches.

🟢 Beginner

Autonomous Basics

Program a simple autonomous that scores 1 pre-loaded Cup and drives to your Alliance Zone. Even a basic autonomous that avoids DQ earns you the Autonomous Bonus if your partner has a better routine. Communication with your alliance partner is key.

🟢 Beginner

Stay Legal, Stay Consistent

Avoid penalties at all costs. Pinning opponents in illegal zones, expanding past size limits, or interference during autonomous results in automatic point deductions. Consistent, clean play is more valuable than risky high-point plays in early qualification matches.

🟢 Beginner

Practice Cup Cycling

Cycling (picking up Cups and scoring them repeatedly) is the primary scoring method. Practice until you can cycle 5+ Cups per minute reliably. Speed and consistency beat complexity every time at the beginner level.

🟡 Intermediate

Dual-Goal Strategy

Score in both your Alliance Goal and one Neutral Goal. This doubles your scoring potential and forces the opponent to defend two locations simultaneously. Requires faster driving and better awareness of field positions.

🟡 Intermediate

Toggle Awareness

Track Toggle states throughout the match. Flip Toggles in the last 30 seconds when the opponent has less time to counter. If you control Toggles, they provide significant bonus points — but they're easy to flip back.

🟡 Intermediate

Scouting for Alliance Selection

Track every team's scoring capabilities, autonomous reliability, and driving skills. The best alliance partner in elimination rounds isn't always the highest scorer — it's the most reliable and complementary to your strategy.

🟡 Intermediate

Midfield Timing

Position your robot at Midfield in the final 10 seconds for bonus points. Pre-plan your endgame sequence — know exactly when to stop cycling and head to Midfield. Practice the timing until it's automatic.

🔴 Advanced

Autonomous Win Point Optimization

Program your autonomous to reliably achieve the Autonomous Win Point (AWP) every match. This requires pre-loading, Touch It Own It (TIOIT), and sometimes sacrificing Autonomous Bonus for AWP. Track opponent autonomous to decide which to attempt each round.

🔴 Advanced

Triple-Goal Dominance

Score in all three Goals (Alliance, Neutral × 2). This is only achievable with high-speed robots (8+ Cups/minute) and exceptional driver skill. Requires a partner who can defend while you cycle aggressively.

🔴 Advanced

Match Scouting & Counter-Strategy

Study your opponent's last 3 matches before facing them. Identify their strategy, weaknesses, and predictable patterns. Adapt your drive routine to counter their specific approach — great teams enter every match with a custom game plan.

🔴 Advanced

Pin Manipulation

Use legal Pins strategically to block opponent's scoring paths for 5-second windows. Time Pin releases to avoid penalties while maximizing field control. Requires precise driving and knowledge of the rulebook.

⚔️ Offensive Playbook

Rapid Cup Cycle

Pick up Cups from the field and score them in Goals as fast as possible. Aim for a 4-second cycle time (2 seconds drive, 1 second score, 1 second return). Practice until the motion is completely automatic.

Pre-Load Advantage

Always use your pre-loaded Cup in the first 5 seconds of driver control or autonomous. Pre-loads are free points — never waste them or score them in the wrong Goal.

Goal Saturation

Score as many Cups as possible in a single Goal before moving to the next. Saturating a Goal (max Cups) prevents the opponent from removing them and securing ownership easily.

🛡️ Defensive Playbook

Body Block on Neutral Goal

Position your robot between the opponent and the Neutral Goal. Make them drive around you, costing them 3-5 seconds per cycle. Even small delays accumulate — 10 blocked cycles = 30-50 seconds of opponent scoring time lost.

Cup Denial

Collect Cups from the field and hold them (without scoring) to prevent the opponent from cycling. Strategic Cup denial can be more valuable than scoring, especially late in matches.

Protected Goal Defense

Stay near Protected Goals (yours) to prevent opponent interference. Many beginner opponents will attempt to score in Protected Goals illegally — your presence discourages this and earns penalties if they do it anyway.

⏱️ Endgame Playbook

The 30-Second Switch

At 30 seconds remaining, mentally switch from "scoring" mode to "endgame" mode. Your priorities change: Toggle control, Midfield position, and opponent denial become more valuable than additional Cup cycles.

Toggle Blitz

Designate one robot to flip all reachable Toggles in the last 15 seconds. Requires a fast, reliable toggle mechanism and pre-planned driving route. Practice until the sequence takes less than 12 seconds.

Midfield Rush

Position in Midfield at exactly 0:05 remaining. Earlier is safer (less risk of missing it) but gives opponent more time to push you out. Later maximizes your cycling time but risks not making it.

Expansion Safety

If your robot has expansion mechanisms, deploy them only in the Expansion Zone and only when scoring is complete. Illegal expansion is an immediate DQ — never rush this action.

🤝 Alliance Coordination Guide

Pre-Match Strategy Meeting (5 Minutes)

Meet with your alliance partner before every match. Agree on: (1) who handles autonomous, (2) which Goals each robot scores in, (3) who defends vs. who offenses, (4) endgame timing signals, (5) what to do if ahead vs. behind.

Communication During Matches

Use the controller rumble feature to signal your partner. Establish codes: 1 rumble = "go to Midfield now", 2 rumbles = "defend the Neutral Goal", 3 rumbles = "we're ahead, play safe." Practice these signals before competition.

Role Division

Robot A (Offensive): Fast cycler, scores as many Cups as possible. Robot B (Support): Scores, defends, flips Toggles, covers Midfield. This division of labor maximizes total alliance score better than both robots doing the same thing.
